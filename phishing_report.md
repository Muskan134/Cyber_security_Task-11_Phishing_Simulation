# Phishing Attack Simulation Report

## 1. Introduction
Phishing is a social engineering cyber attack where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords,
OTPs, and bank details. This report explains a simulated phishing attack, how it works, how to detect it, and how to prevent it.  
This simulation is created only for educational and awareness purposes.

---

## 2. Objective of the Task
- To understand phishing attacks
- To learn how phishing emails are created
- To identify phishing red flags
- To learn phishing prevention techniques
- To spread cybersecurity awareness

---

## 3. What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyber attack in which fake emails, messages, or websites are used to impersonate trusted organizations in order to steal sensitive user information.

---

## 4. Types of Phishing Attacks
- Email Phishing  
- SMS Phishing (Smishing)  
- Voice Phishing (Vishing)  
- Website Phishing  
- Social Media Phishing  

---

## 5. Phishing Email Simulation

### Sample Phishing Email
**Subject:** Urgent! Verify Your Account Immediately

Dear User,

We noticed suspicious activity in your account.
Please verify your account immediately to avoid account suspension.

Click the link below to verify:
http://fake-verification-link.com

Regards,
Security Team


⚠️ This email is created only for simulation and learning purposes.

---

## 6. Landing Page Concept
In real phishing attacks, attackers create fake login pages that look like genuine websites (such as banks or email services). 
When users enter their login details, attackers capture this information.

In this task, the landing page concept is explained theoretically without creating or deploying any real phishing website.

---

## 7. User Response Analysis
- Clicking on suspicious links may lead to data theft
- Entering credentials on fake websites compromises accounts
- Ignoring or reporting suspicious emails helps prevent attacks

---

## 8. Phishing Red Flags Identification
- Unknown or suspicious sender
- Urgent or threatening language
- Requests for sensitive information
- Spelling or grammatical errors
- Suspicious links or fake domains

---

## 9. Prevention and Safety Measures
- Never click on unknown email links
- Always verify the sender’s email address
- Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
- Keep systems and browsers updated
- Use antivirus and spam filters
- Educate users about phishing attacks

---

## 10. Conclusion
Phishing attacks are a serious cybersecurity threat that can lead to data breaches and financial loss. Awareness, cautious behavior,
and proper security practices are the best defenses against phishing attacks.
